Look, there is a difference between capitalism, socialism, communism vs democracy and dictatorship. We have term limits in the US Presidency.

I seem to be on the TABOO side of everything. Communism is a system that can be leveraged in a war-time rationing kind of way. It seems that our electronic systems are setup to handle that transition smoothly.

If we have to ratchet down industrialism, and there are many possible reasons we may have to, then a communistic economy makes a lot of sense. Simply put, we can keep prices stable with that kind of system. Without that, we could be in a position of a bloody fight for every last item. ( Think stores like Home Depot and Lowes being almost entirely out of items right away and then having to deal with the grocery store and getting a new car. My uncle was a big time economics person. )

I tend to be stand-offish on Socialism which is a major reason I am now registered as a Republican. If we go into a period of drawdown where employment rapidly drops because of reduced physical goods on the market, then the idea of "strong unions and healthcare" may not work so well because of transportation issues and other raw goods sourcing. It's a complicated mix/problem that I personally feel President Trump is working out IN A MAJOR WAY. I get energized thinking about being in the position to work on that plan as the next US President. If it is needed of course.
