Religion and Freedom

2026-06-17    01:43:36 AM

Hello! My religion which I describe as "solitary mediation" appears to be at odds with the majority of society. I'm not a parent that I know of and I'm not based on sports or competition. My mother in fact was very against competition. I was raised in a strict atheist houshold where it was forbidden to talk about religion.

I've been exposed to many religions in my life. Through former wives and musical people, I was exposed to several different churches which I have recently formally rejected. Unfortunately, but I had to do that to be true to my concept of God.

I was sold on the concept of religious freedom and representation in our United States Government. Article VI and The First Amendment are formed to protect relgious beliefs and practices. Churches set up 501c3's so they can function as charities legally. I've only recently become aware of all of this. I'd setup a 501c3 for my solitary meditation religion, but I simply do not have the $600 (plus other fees) to file for it. The up side to having a religious 501c3 is naming a beneficiary 501c3 in case of my death.

It could be that I religiously operate like a transwoman Nun? It's weird like that and I went into a Catholic Church in about 2010 to a confession and declared that I do not see myself as Catholic or Buddhist. I then got involved in some Baptist communities (mostly against my will), but that was not my true religion. In fact, it was kind of in the opposite direction.

----

I'm more of a worker about religion. And perhaps that is what the RUSH song "The Working Man" is about? I mean, Neil Peart did not write the lyrics to that, so perhaps it was more of a Geddy Lee song?

I'm just very much about the God of physical activity with the non-living. You know, work! Like doing the dishes or assembling LCD televisions. The problem that I have been having over and over in my life is being hazed on the job. I even wrote a letter to President Trump a few weeks ago talking about one ultra-ridiculous job I was doing and said to him "Do people want the job to get done or are they more interested in being social and pushing people around?"

I suppose I've become scared of all my former relationships with people. I'm scared that we don't see eye to eye on these matters. I'm scared that they are scared of me and that is why I'm being hazed so much. I appear to be heavily challenged to drink a beer for my own health. AND IT MAY BE SOUND MEDICAL ADVICE! And that is a huge problem with me right now. I was brought up in a clean and sober household. My dad joined Alcoholics Anonymous BEFORE I WAS BORN in 1971 and didn't drink at all during my life. Unless he and my mom were lying to me, which they may have been. And that is disturbing.

----

I don't know what to do about this dilemma. I'm trying hard to find some peace with it and my neighbors. My neighbors are actually amazingly cool! But there is also this huge religious conflict and I must stick up for myself. I don't know what my next move will be. I may drink a beer alone. I guess that makes the most sense for some reason? The concept of if I can just buy some beer and drink one, then people will come around to either stop hazing me or actively start accepting me.

It's a messed up conflict, but it is a thing and I'm doing my best with it. And these are strange times to be living in. Absolutely! Wireless communications, computers, advanced chemistry. So much stuff and our human work is contracting now because we can't keep this pace up forever. And it appears that my life is a huge part of this political reality or fix. It's a lot of pressure.
