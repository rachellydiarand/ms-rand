Many decades ago I became focused on the Haber-Bosch process which uses a catalyst to break apart atmospheric Nitrogen and fix it to the Hydrogen component of methane (natural gas). Some sources say it uses an iron catalyst while others say nickel. It's apparently done in a pressurized system. It makes amonia (the blue stuff) and that is then used to make nitrogen fertilizer.

What I read last week about Carl Bosch is that his process which he developed from 1900 to 1910 "uses 1% of humanity's energy and is responsibile for keeping half the planet alive". Something like that. He was born and lived in Germany. The article I read said that he was a Nazi dissenter and that because he opposed the Nazis, he was essentially let go / fired from his own company and died somewhere between 1930 and 1940.

The other two compounds in the three compound common fertilizer are something we dig up apparently. The nitrogen component is very high tech and is the single thing that allowed us to grow so big in population to build all this stuff. We wouldn't have gotten this big and advanced without it.
