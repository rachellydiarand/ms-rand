In the Pacific NW, we don't have a lot of natural disasters. I served in the US Navy in Memphis TN and Norfolk VA. My military background is pretty massive even though I simply played trombone and bass with the Navy.

* I shipped off to boot camp on 11-17-1992, right after Bill Clinton was elected. I was 3.5 months shy of my 22nd birthday. I was awarded "Honor Recruit" in boot camp which gave me a paygrade upgrade to E-3. I realized recently that I may have gotten Honor Recruit because I had written a 157 page jazz transcription book the previous year and that may have been some form of honorary degree. I've certainly done much, much more self education than that since then with all my software, math and political work.

I've been through several hurricanes. I know you all have your community connections and I identify more with being a generic "world citizen". My music spans many styles and my personal life is highly based on sitting, solitary meditation.

I'm accustomed to having US Navy Seals living next door and their training base behind my house. Accustomed to walking on aircraft carriers to play music on their textured decks and in the hanger. Accustomed to being on a US Navy Destroyer doing battle manuevers and stowing gear in the plane cargo hold and attaching the net so that the gear doesn't fall in on the hatch during flight.

Fork lifting gear into the side of a plane.

Skipping customs.

I am NOT accustomed to taking normal hotel vacations or using my civilian passport.

The US Navy and US Marines have a strong bond between them. The US Army is slightly different. It is my understanding that the Army is more in the job of on the ground help with sandbags, temporary shelters and meals while the US Marines are more in the job of protecting the President and doing special ops. I've been focusing on the Marines more and more lately. Interesting thing they do.

I spend a lot of time doing some virtual disaster planning stuff with the extreme poverty I've been through. Living in cars EXTENSIVELY. And on the streets. EXTENSIVELY. Sometimes it seems like my life goes from one disaster to the next, but I had some pretty good years in my 20's and 40's.

Simply put, I know what it FEELS like to be suffering. I have massive compassion for all people on this level. As an elected president, I can help out in a multitude of ways.
