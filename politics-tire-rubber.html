Tire Rubber

2026-06-15    15:04:06 PM

OK, the tire rubber situation. I read a Scientific American article that stated that someone at Polymer Solutions in Blacksburg Virginia stated that "Tire rubber is more complex than one may realize. A car tire has about 40 different compounds in it. By weight, the average car tire has:

28% Natural Rubber (latex sap)
28% Synthetic Rubber (from oil, kinda like toothpaste tubes most likely - Rachel)
28% Black Carbon Filler (a reinforcing agent)"

28% x 3 = 84%

The theory I worked up on that is that the sap from one Brazilian Rubber Tree can make one common car tire a year. So if you replace your tires every 4 years, you'll need one tree of latex sap to keep your car on the road.

We are obviously using plenty of tires right now, but it certainly can't last forever. It's kind of like banana trees, we are exporting a lot of material OUT of those environments and that environment "has changed" because of that. You can search the internet for a video called "A Brief History of Rubber" to learn more, or other sources of course.
